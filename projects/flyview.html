Flyview - Fly Over France


Worked as Software Engineer: Unreal Engine C++ & VR

The project was in Virtual Reality in a room where people get into jetpack-like seats and fly over France. My role was to adapt the movement of the player to the movement of the character, create paths the player could choose and manage collisions of the player.

Network connection

The movements of the character are replicated in real life. It is done by connecting the application to a seat stylized as a jetpack. The seat can move with four pneumatic cylinders to which the instructions are sent. Moreover, a fan is pulsing air to the player depending on the flying speed. The information is sent to the devices through the network. I conceived solutions for network communication and created testing software to check the connection states.

Git responsabilities

As the main developer on the project, I was the one in charge of managing the git contributions of the team. I've handled merging, rebasing and history rewriting depending on the needs of the team.

Debug systems

To facilitate the development of the game, I created debug and visualization systems. They show the possible trajectories of the player, or the collisions added to the objects in game. Below are some of those representations.

Visualization of the volume where the player evolves. It changes over time and can be visualized using a Level Sequence. It is dynamic and changes in real time, following the desired modifications.

Visualization of path selection. At some point the player can choose a path. This tool uses the previous one to show both paths and where they diverge and converge back.

Visualization of collision boxes used to detect collisions between the player and the terrain. The future position of the player is calculated and if a collision is detected the trajectory is re-routed.

User Interface

I have worked on the user interface. It mostly consisted of counters to show the speed of the player and the number of rings collected during the flight.

User interface the player can see in the headset. Speed counter on the left, ring counter on the right, current altitude in the middle with the name of the current place.

Splines showing the possible paths for the player. They adapt their shape to the position of the player and the position of the rings.
